Our Story

a riad on main street

Step through the door at 118 South Main and you leave Ann Arbor behind. Three floors unfold like a Moroccan riad — the blue-and-white mosaic tile fountain greets you first, its soft murmur setting the tempo. Hanging greenery spills from every rail, exposed brick warms the walls, and the faint hum of Arabic music drifts through the airy, loft-like space.

Climb the motion-sensor-lit stairs to the mezzanine or the top floor, each level revealing a different vantage of the same feeling: that you have traveled somewhere far without ever leaving downtown. This is not a themed corner or an accent wall. It is a full, three-story immersion in Moroccan hospitality — built so you can taste it, see it, and sit inside it.
